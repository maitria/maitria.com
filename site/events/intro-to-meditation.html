meditation for geeks


Why mindfulness?

And why for geeks?

Programmers and other humans meditate for lots of reasons. Here are some: stress relief, emotional awareness and comfort, more compassion, healthier focus and attention, greater empathy, mindfulness, body awareness, self-compassion.

Come join a small group of geeks, and begin or strengthen your own meditation practice.


How it works

What's involved?

  • weekly live video chat via hangouts
  • online learning community
  • one-on-one instruction opportunities

Once you're signed up, you'll receive an email with information about signing up for the online community. Here, you'll find out about each week's topic, resources like audio files and readings, and you'll be able to log your sitting practice and post questions or thoughts that come up.

In the weekly sessions, we'll typically meditate together for 20 minutes, followed by Q&A, After a brief talk on a relevant topic, the instructor will introduce a supplemental technique, for the following week's practice.


experienced instruction

Alex Harms

Alex began studying mindfulness techniques since 1985, and began teaching in 2007. She has studied formal logic, zen buddhism, humanistic psychology, and sat with teachers ranging from Marshal Rosenberg and Conal Elliott to Neale Donald Walsh and Kim Krishbaum. Alex's practice is grounded zen buddhism, with focus on cultivating curiosity, attention to body sensations, awareness of emotions, and an unflinching willingness to look at reality without flinching.
