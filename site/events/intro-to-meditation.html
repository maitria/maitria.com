meditation for geeks


Why mindfulness?

And why for geeks?

Meditation practice has made a big difference for us, and we'd like to offer to share that with you.

Everybody knows meditation can help with stress relief and has health benefits. But there are things we've found especially useful for us, as geeks. Mindfulness work has helped us with...

  • becoming more comfortable with our emotions
  • developing compassion
  • achieving healthier focus
  • control of our attention
  • getting better at empathy
  • gaining awareness of our bodies
  • learning self-compassion
  • improving relationships

We'd like to invite you to join our six-week online class. Weekly group instruction and meditation sessions will happen via Hangouts, and throughout the course you'll have an online community for questions and thoughts that come to you between sessions. Together with a small group of other technical folks, you'll learn meditation basics and intermediate techniques, and begin to form habits that can stick with you.


How it works

What's involved?

  • weekly live video chat via hangouts
  • online learning community
  • one-on-one instruction opportunities

Once you're signed up, you'll receive an email with information about signing up for the online community. Here, you'll find out about each week's topic, resources like audio files and readings, and you'll be able to log your sitting practice and post questions or thoughts that come up.

In the weekly sessions, we'll typically meditate together for 20 minutes, followed by Q&A, After a brief talk on a relevant topic, the instructor will introduce a supplemental technique, for the following week's practice.


experienced instruction

Alex Harms

Classes will be led by Alex Harms. Alex has been studying mindfulness techniques since 1985, and began teaching in 2007. She has studied formal logic, zen buddhism, humanistic psychology, with teachers including a Japanese trained zen monk*, Marshall Rosenberg, Conal Elliott, Neale Donald Walsh, and Kim Krishbaum. Alex's practice is grounded zen buddhism, with focus on cultivating curiosity, attention to body sensations, awareness of emotions, and a willingness to look at reality without flinching.
